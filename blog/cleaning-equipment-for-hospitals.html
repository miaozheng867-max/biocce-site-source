Choosing Cleaning Equipment for Hospitals and Healthcare Facilities

Healthcare facilities have the most demanding cleaning requirements of any industry. Patient safety, infection control, and regulatory compliance all depend on effective cleaning.

Infection Control Requirements

Healthcare-acquired infections (HAIs) affect millions of patients annually. Proper cleaning and disinfection of floors, surfaces, and equipment is critical. Floor scrubbers and vacuums used in healthcare must not spread contaminants.

Key Equipment for Healthcare Cleaning

Low-noise floor scrubbers: Hospital corridors require cleaning during all hours. The BC500 walk-behind scrubber operates at low noise levels suitable for patient areas.

HEPA-filtered vacuums: High-efficiency particulate air (HEPA) filters capture 99.97% of particles down to 0.3 microns. The BV30 vacuum with optional HEPA filtration is ideal for healthcare use.

Surgical suite equipment: Operating rooms require specialized cleaning protocols. Dedicated equipment with antimicrobial surfaces and HEPA exhaust filtration.

Hygienic Machine Design

Look for: smooth, easy-to-clean surfaces, removable and cleanable tanks, sealed electrical components (for wet cleaning), antimicrobial additive in plastic components, and tool-free brush and squeegee changes.

Training and Protocols

Healthcare cleaning staff require specialized training: color-coded cleaning tools to prevent cross-contamination between areas, proper dilution of cleaning chemicals, correct machine operation and maintenance, and documentation of cleaning activities.

Regulatory Compliance

Healthcare facilities must comply with local health department regulations, Joint Commission standards (US), and HTM guidelines (UK). Ensure your cleaning equipment supplier understands healthcare compliance requirements.
