How to Start a Cleaning Equipment Distribution Business

Starting a cleaning equipment distribution business is a proven path to entrepreneurship in a growing market. The global cleaning equipment market is projected to reach $50+ billion by 2030. Here is how to get started.

1. Market Research

Identify your target market: which industries in your region need cleaning equipment? Warehouses, hospitals, hotels, retail, food processing? Analyze competitors and their pricing. Identify gaps in the market that you can fill.

2. Choose Your Supplier

The most critical decision. Look for manufacturers with: proven export experience (50+ countries), flexible OEM/private label options, competitive pricing without sacrificing quality, reliable after-sales support, and fast lead times. BIOCCE offers all of these advantages.

3. Decide Your Business Model

Distributor: Buy and resell established brands. Lower risk, lower margin.
Private label: Sell equipment under your own brand. Higher margin, more work (branding, certification).
Hybrid: Offer both branded and private label options to different customer segments.

4. Set Up Operations

Secure warehouse space for inventory. Set up a website and sales materials. Establish service and repair capabilities. Hire trained sales and technical staff. Obtain necessary business licenses and insurance.

5. Build Your Sales Pipeline

Target facility managers, procurement departments, and cleaning contractors. Offer demonstration events. Provide exceptional customer service. Build relationships with local cleaning contractors who need reliable equipment.
