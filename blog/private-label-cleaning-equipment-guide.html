Private Label Cleaning Equipment: A Complete Guide for Distributors

Private labeling cleaning equipment is a proven strategy for distributors to build their brand without investing in manufacturing. This guide covers everything you need to know.

What Is Private Label Cleaning Equipment?

Also known as white-label or OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturing), this arrangement lets you sell cleaning machines under your own brand name while a manufacturer like BIOCCE handles production, quality control, and compliance.

Customization Options

Branding – Custom logos, color schemes, and decals applied to the machine.
Packaging – Retail-ready boxes with your brand, product images, and specifications.
Controls and Labels – Control panels in your local language with your brand name.
Specifications – Voltage, plug type, motor power, and battery configuration adapted to your market.
Documentation – User manuals, technical drawings, and compliance certificates under your brand.

MOQ and Lead Times

Typical MOQ for private label cleaning equipment is 10-50 units per model. Lead times range from 15-45 days depending on customization complexity and order volume.

Quality Assurance

Reputable OEM manufacturers provide: pre-shipment inspection reports (with photos/video), sample approval before mass production, factory audit access, and warranty support for end customers.

Certifications

Ensure your OEM partner provides: CE certification (European market), EAC certification (Eurasian customs union), or UL certification (North America) as required by your target market.
