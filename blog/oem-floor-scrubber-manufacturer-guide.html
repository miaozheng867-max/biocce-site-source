OEM Guide

How to Choose an OEM Floor Scrubber Manufacturer in China

For distributors and cleaning equipment brands worldwide, finding the right OEM manufacturer is one of the most critical business decisions you will make. The right partner ensures consistent product quality, competitive pricing, and reliable after-sales support that protects your brand reputation.

China has become the global manufacturing hub for floor scrubbers and cleaning equipment. With hundreds of factories to choose from, knowing how to evaluate and select the right OEM partner is essential. This guide will walk you through the key factors to consider.

Why Choose Chinese OEM for Floor Scrubbers?

Chinese manufacturers now produce over 70% of the world's floor cleaning equipment. The advantages go beyond lower labor costs:

  • Complete supply chain integration — Motors, batteries, brushes, and electronic components are all produced domestically, reducing lead times and cost
  • Rapid prototyping and customization — Chinese factories can produce custom molds and branding within 15-30 days
  • Scalable production — Whether you need 10 units or 10,000, capable factories can scale to meet demand
  • Export expertise — Experienced OEM exporters understand international certifications, packaging standards, and shipping documentation

Key Factors to Evaluate an OEM Partner

1. Production Capability
Visit the factory or request a video tour. Look for modern assembly lines, QC testing stations, and organized inventory management. A factory like BIOCCE with dedicated production lines for walk-behind and ride-on models demonstrates specialization.

2. Quality Control Processes
Ask about their QC checkpoints. Reliable manufacturers test every machine before shipment — motor load, battery cycle, water leak detection, and safety compliance. Request their QC checklist and test reports.

3. Customization Options
The best OEM partners offer flexible customization: custom RAL colors, your logo and branding, different voltage configurations (110V/220V), battery type options (lithium or lead-acid), and custom packaging for your market.

4. Minimum Order Quantities (MOQ)
For new distributors, high MOQs create unnecessary risk. Look for partners willing to start with 1-2 trial units. BIOCCE welcomes trial orders, allowing you to test the market before committing to larger volumes.

5. After-Sales Support
A reliable OEM partner provides: spare parts inventory, video-guided troubleshooting, remote technical support, and warranty service. This protects your brand when end customers need help.

6. Communication and Language
English proficiency matters. Clear communication prevents costly misunderstandings about specifications, packaging, and shipping. Russian language support is a plus for distributors targeting Central Asian markets.
